Warning Signs to Watch For

Emergency Warning Signs

Call emergency services (911) immediately if experiencing: severe chest pain, difficulty breathing, sudden weakness/numbness, severe bleeding, loss of consciousness, or signs of stroke (facial drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty).

Early Warning Signs of Diabetes

Type 2 diabetes often develops slowly. Recognizing early signs can lead to earlier diagnosis and treatment.

Increased Thirst and Frequent Urination

Excess sugar in your blood causes fluid to be pulled from tissues, making you feel thirsty. You may drink and urinate more than usual.

Unexplained Weight Loss

When you lose glucose through frequent urination, you also lose calories. You might lose weight even though you're eating normally.

Blurred Vision

If your blood sugar is too high, fluid may be pulled from the lenses of your eyes, affecting your ability to focus clearly.

Action Steps

If you experience any of these symptoms, especially multiple symptoms, schedule a blood glucose test with your healthcare provider.

Early Warning Signs of Heart Disease

Heart disease can develop silently over years. These signs indicate you should see a cardiologist.

Chest Discomfort or Pain (Angina)

Pressure, tightness, or pain in your chest that may spread to your shoulders, arms, neck, jaw, or back.

Shortness of Breath

Difficulty breathing or feeling winded during normal activities or at rest.

Irregular Heartbeat

Heart palpitations, fluttering, or feeling like your heart is racing or skipping beats.

Urgent: Heart Attack Warning Signs

Call 911 immediately if experiencing: Severe chest pain or pressure, pain radiating to arm/jaw, shortness of breath, cold sweats, nausea, or lightheadedness.

Cancer Warning Signs to Watch For

While these symptoms can have many causes, persistent symptoms warrant medical evaluation.

Unexplained Weight Loss

Losing 10 pounds or more without trying.

Persistent Cough or Hoarseness

A cough that lasts more than a few weeks.

Unusual Bleeding or Discharge

Blood in urine, stool, or when coughing; unusual vaginal bleeding or discharge.

Lump or Thickening

Any new lump in breast, testicle, lymph nodes, or elsewhere in the body.

Remember: Early Detection Saves Lives

Persistent symptoms lasting more than 2-3 weeks should be evaluated by a healthcare provider. When cancer is caught early, treatment is often more successful.

Why Early Detection Matters

• Cancer: 5-year survival rates can exceed 90% when caught early, compared to less than 30% for late-stage detection

• Diabetes: Early diagnosis and management can prevent or delay serious complications like kidney disease, blindness, and amputations

• Heart Disease: Early intervention can prevent heart attacks, reduce the need for invasive procedures, and significantly extend life expectancy
